# Source: Gen Z Churn Analysis

**Researcher:** Shira | **Date:** November 2025 | **Sample:** n=500 Gen Z females (18–27) who churned from Facetune

61% churned within the first 6 months. Three drivers are statistically tied for top position: authenticity concerns (21.6%), stopped editing face/body (20.2%), and found better alternatives (20%). Churned users migrated to TikTok (60.5%), CapCut (58.5%), and VSCO (43.1%). Alternatives win on ease, capability, cost, and lighting/filter quality — not just face-specific features.

## Churn Drivers (Top to Bottom)

| Driver | % | Insight |
|---|---|---|
| Authenticity concerns | 21.6% | Fear of appearing fake or being called out |
| No longer edit face/body | 20.2% | Shifted away from face-specific editing |
| Found better alternatives | 20% | Another app solved their needs better |
| Too expensive | 15.6% | Subscription cost not justified |
| Too difficult | 12.8% | Feature complexity or UX friction |
| Didn't like quality | 9% | Results didn't meet expectations |

## Timing: The 6-Month Cliff

**61%** churn within first 6 months. This is a critical retention window — new users either stick through the paywall or abandon. High early-stage friction is the likely culprit.

## Where Churned Users Go

| Platform | % Migrated To |
|---|---|
| TikTok | 60.5% |
| CapCut | 58.5% |
| VSCO | 43.1% |

**Multi-app behavior:** Users don't replace FT with one app; they distribute editing across 2–4 apps. CapCut's dominance is notable (video + editing integrated).

## Feature Usage Among Churned Users

Most-used features before leaving:

| Feature | % |
|---|---|
| Skin smoothing | 42.8% |
| Lighting/Color | 38% |
| Face adjustments | 31.2% |

**Insight:** Churned users were engaged with core features but still left. This is not a usage problem — it's a motivation/value problem.

## Why Alternatives Are Better

Among churned users, top reasons alternative apps won:

| Reason | % |
|---|---|
| Easier to use | 25% |
| Has needed capabilities | 24% |
| Better lighting/filters | 17% |
| Free or low-cost | 17% |

## Authenticity Sentiment (Two Views)

Among churned users concerned about authenticity (n=108):

| Sentiment | % |
|---|---|
| Prefer natural appearance | 38.9% |
| Results look unrealistic | 34.3% |

**Two problems:** (1) Users are shifting toward natural aesthetics, (2) FT's output reads as "overdone" or "fake-looking" to some users.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey + open-ended feedback
- **Participants:** 500 Gen Z females (18–27) who churned from Facetune
- **Definition of churn:** Stopped app usage for 30+ days
- **Measures:** Churn reasons, timing, migration patterns, feature usage, authenticity concerns
- **Date:** November 2025

## Implications for Retention

1. **Authenticity positioning** — Reposition toward enhancement, not transformation
2. **Early paywall** — The 6-month cliff suggests paywall friction in months 1–3
3. **Lighting features** — 38% used lighting; users want this — double down
4. **Multi-app reality** — Don't fight it; design for it (share to other apps, import from others)
