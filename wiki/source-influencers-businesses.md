# Source: Influencers & Business Users

**Researcher:** Magali | **Date:** August 2024 | **Sample:** n=17 interviews

Professional creators have fundamentally shifted their editing workflow away from heavy face/body editing toward lighting, body positioning, and video. They perceive Facetune as a face-only tool and work around that limitation. Influencers juggle 3–5 apps per project and are frustrated by subscription fragmentation.

## The Editing Shift

**From Heavy Editing to Lighting**
Content creators no longer rely on skin smoothing and reshaping. Instead, they prioritize lighting (golden hour, ring lights, post-production color grading) as the foundation of their aesthetic.

**From Selfies to Full-Body & Video**
Portfolio evolution: selfies and close-ups are replaced by full-body shots, environmental context, and short-form video. The editing need has moved beyond the face.

**Heavy Filters Out, Aesthetic Consistency In**
Old editing: stacked filters, heavy adjustments. Current workflow: cohesive color grade, consistent temperature and tone across posts. Subtle over obvious.

## Key Problem: "Facetune = Face Only"

Influencers believe Facetune handles face enhancement exclusively. They use it for occasional touch-ups but view it as limited for their full-workflow needs. This perception drives them to alternatives and supplementary apps.

## Current Workflow Stack

- **VSCO/Taza** — Filters and color grading (not Facetune)
- **Lightroom** — Lighting adjustments, tone, saturation
- **Canva** — Layout, design, text, templates
- **Facetune** — Occasional spot retouching (if they remember it exists)

## Subscription Frustration

35% manage 3+ subscription services per month. Multiple paywall renewals create decision fatigue. Influencers request bundled or suite pricing to simplify management. Free alternatives (especially CapCut) are gaining adoption.

## Methodology

- **Type:** In-depth interviews
- **Participants:** 17 influencers and professional content creators (mix of follower counts and niches)
- **Topics:** Editing workflow, tool preferences, pain points, subscription preferences, competitive app awareness
- **Date:** August 2024

## Recommendations from Users

1. **AI personalization** — Auto-detect subject and optimize settings per content type
2. **Natural look tools** — Deemphasize face-reshaping; lead with enhancement
3. **Expand beyond face/body** — Lighting, color, background, environmental tools
4. **Bulk editing** — Apply settings to multiple photos at once (signature style)
