# Source: User Personas Survey

**Researcher:** Magali Lifschitz | **Date:** June 2025 | **Sample:** n=1,000 survey

Six behavioral personas define the Facetune user base. Users often exhibit traits from multiple personas — the percentages reflect primary and secondary characteristics, not mutually exclusive segments.

## The Six Personas

| Persona | Primary Driver | Sample Size |
|---------|---|---|
| [[persona-nicole-beauty-driven-millennial]] | Skin enhancement, portraits | 47% |
| [[persona-zoe-gen-z-selective]] | Authenticity-focused, minimal editing | 18% |
| [[persona-jordan-creative-experimenter]] | Feature exploration, effects | 28% |
| [[persona-annie-memory-documenter]] | Photo preservation | 26% |
| [[persona-rachel-family-photographer]] | Family photos, group shots | 9% |
| [[persona-alex-influencer-business]] | Content production, monetization | 22% |

## Key Insight

Nicole (Beauty-Driven Millennial) dominates the user base at 47%, making skin smoothing the core app driver. However, secondary personas reveal three distinct editing mindsets — beauty-focused (Nicole), authenticity-conscious (Zoe), and creative explorers (Jordan) — that require different feature emphasis and onboarding approaches.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey
- **Participants:** 1,000 Facetune users
- **Scope:** Usage patterns, feature adoption, motivations, demographics
- **Coverage:** Represents existing user base, not app store universe

## Notable Patterns

- **Overlapping traits** — A user may be "primarily Nicole" but show "secondary Annie" behaviors (preserving important moments)
- **Authenticity spectrum** — Ranges from Zoe's "edits look fake" anxiety to Nicole's "I want to look my best" acceptance
- **Feature interest** — Varies significantly by persona; skin smoothing dominates Nicole/Rachel, creative effects drive Jordan/Alex
