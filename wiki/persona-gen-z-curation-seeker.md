# Persona: Gen Z Curation Seeker

> **Note:** This archetype is based on a small qualitative sample (n=6 in-depth interviews). It represents an observed pattern, not a statistically validated segment. See [[source-gen-z-in-depth-research]] for methodology.

Curation Seekers are Gen Z users obsessed with aesthetic consistency and feed cohesion. They spend significant time selecting, editing, and curating their social presence. They represent a distinct behavioral archetype within Gen Z — driven by the desire to build a visually consistent personal brand, not by self-expression or authenticity concerns (like Expressive Artists) but by control and craftsmanship.

## The Core Motivation

The Curation Seeker treats their Instagram (or TikTok) feed like an art installation. They plan color palettes, themes, and aesthetic arcs. They'll spend an hour on a single post, trying different filters, color grades, and crops to match their feed's existing mood. For them, curation isn't vanity — it's a form of creative control and identity expression. They want their feed to tell a story, and every photo needs to fit that narrative.

## User Profiles: Ava & Ella

**Ava: The Perfectionist**
- Spends ~1 hour editing a single post
- Works with photo dumps and collages to curate sets
- Uses multiple apps per photo (Facetune → VSCO → another app)
- Highly perfectionistic; deletes posts that don't fit the feed aesthetic
- Creates artificial cohesion through color grading and cropping

**Ella: The Selective Curator**
- Primarily posts to Instagram, occasionally TikTok
- Edits every other post (not every photo she takes)
- Uses the same multi-app workflow as Ava
- Thoughtful about which moments make the feed
- Values consistency but less obsessive than Ava

Both share: they want creative control without grunt work. They're willing to spend time, but they want powerful tools that make the work feel intentional, not tedious.

## Key Behaviors

- **Spends significant time per edit**: Curation Seekers aren't quick-fix users. They'll spend 30 minutes to an hour crafting a single post.
- **Stacks multiple apps**: They use Facetune for portraits, VSCO or Lightroom for color grading, and sometimes Canva or Photoshop for collages. App-switching is normal.
- **Photo dump and collage users**: They might take 50 photos on a day out and create a 4-6 image collage to post. Editing the collage is the creative work.
- **Selective about what makes the feed**: Not every good photo goes up. They curate ruthlessly. If a photo doesn't fit the aesthetic, it doesn't post.
- **Color and tone obsessed**: They spend time color-grading to match their existing feed. Consistency matters more than individual photo quality.
- **Long-term feed planning**: Curation Seekers think about their feed as a whole. They might plan next week's posts to maintain flow and rhythm.

## Pain Points

- **App fragmentation**: They want to do everything in one app. Having to switch between Facetune, VSCO, and another tool breaks the creative flow.
- **Lack of advanced color controls**: If a tool doesn't let them match their feed's color grade precisely, they have to use another app. Facetune's creative effects are good, but color matching is weak.
- **Bulk editing limitations**: They might want to apply one color grade to a set of photos to maintain consistency. Current tools don't handle this efficiently.
- **Preview limitations**: They want to see how a photo will look in their feed before posting (i.e., how it looks next to their other photos, given feed dimensions and placement). Most apps don't offer this.
- **Undo/revision difficulty**: If they post and then realize the edit doesn't quite fit, they want to revise. Facetune exports and doesn't allow easy revision without re-editing.
- **Perfectionistic paralysis**: Curation Seekers can get stuck, unable to decide between edit options. Tools that help them decide faster would reduce friction.

## Comparison to Other Segments

- **vs. Zoe (Gen-Z Selective)**: Both are Gen Z, but Zoe is authenticity-conscious and edits sparingly. Curation Seekers are aesthetically obsessed and edit extensively.
- **vs. Expressive Artist**: Both are creative Gen Z users, but Expressive Artists edit for fun and self-expression. Curation Seekers edit for control and feed consistency.
- **vs. Nicole (Beauty-Driven Millennial)**: Nicole has one goal (smooth skin). Curation Seekers are multi-tool, multi-step editors with aesthetic goals.
- **vs. Jordan (Creative Experimenter)**: Jordan explores for fun; Curation Seekers curate intentionally. Different creative motivation.

## Retention & Growth Opportunities

- **All-in-one creative editing**: Integrate color grading, cropping, collage-making, and portrait editing in one tool. Reduce app-switching friction.
- **Feed preview feature**: Show users how their edited photo will look in their actual feed (respecting dimensions, placement, surrounding photos).
- **Batch color grading**: Let Curation Seekers apply one color grade to multiple photos at once. This would save hours per session.
- **Advanced color and tone controls**: Offer selective color adjustment, HSL sliders, and curves (like Lightroom) within Facetune. Let them color-match precisely.
- **Collage and multi-photo layouts**: If Curation Seekers can build collages within Facetune, they stay in-app longer and reduce tool-switching.
- **Save and reuse presets**: Let them save their feed's color grade as a preset and apply it to future photos. Build preset marketplace so they can discover other feeds' looks.
- **Undo/revision after export**: Let them import a previously exported photo and revise the edits without starting from scratch.
