## Research Findings

*Insights from user studies, usability tests, surveys, and interviews.*

- [[lighting-universal-editing-need]] — Lighting is the #1 editing need across every segment, every device, every study
- [[skin-smoothing-differentiator]] — FT's #1 differentiating feature (+27.6 pts vs non-FT), but tied to authenticity tension
- [[mobile-vs-desktop-editing]] — Phone = convenience, desktop = precision; 14% already cross-device; desktop FT opportunity
- [[subscription-friction]] — Paywall confusion, trial cycling workarounds, contributes to churn (15.6%)
- [[gen-z-churn-analysis]] — 61% churn in 6 months; top 3 drivers statistically tied at ~20% each
- [[onboarding-experience]] — Onboarding is simple but fails to create emotional connection or show personalization payoff; Persona concept strongest

## Concepts & Frameworks

*UX frameworks, mental models, and recurring themes across research.*

- [[authenticity-and-editing]] — THE central tension across all FT research; 21.6% of churn, brand stigma, detection fear
- [[editing-tool-ecosystem]] — Users stack 2-4 apps; native first, then specialized tools; FT = narrow "face/body fixes" role
- [[detection-anxiety]] — "Can someone tell I edited this?" — dominant male concern, likely extends beyond
- [[ai-editing-expectations]] — Users want "fix my photo" but AI delivers "changed my face"; identity accuracy is the blocker
- [[facetune-brand-perception]] — Dual problem: awareness gap (most non-users unaware) + perception problem ("fake/catfish")
