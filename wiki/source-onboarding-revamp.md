# Source: Onboarding Revamp Usability Test

**Team:** Facetune Design Group & UX Research | **Date:** April 2026 | **Sample:** n=120 (40 per concept), 18-40, non-FT users, 50/50 gender split | **Method:** Unmoderated usability testing + ranking via UserTesting.com with Figma prototypes

Three onboarding concepts were tested against each other to find a more engaging, modern, interactive first experience. The Persona concept emerged as the strongest candidate — praised as creative and delightful — though no statistically significant quantitative differences were found between any of the three concepts.

## The Three Concepts

### Baseline (Current)
A 3-question, skippable flow with a step counter (1/3).

**Strengths:** Simplicity is the #1 strength. Users praised it as short, simple, and easy. Clean, modern design with large tap targets.

**Weaknesses:** Gender question felt uncomfortable — nearly half of participants called it intrusive, confusing, or irrelevant. No visible personalization payoff (users didn't understand why they were being asked). Low engagement ceiling — about a third rated engagement 2-3/5. Efficient but not memorable.

### Persona Concept
A flow-chart navigation experience with a personality quiz that delivers an "editing personality type" result.

**Strengths:** Sparked delight — praised as "unique," "creative," "like a journey." Strong emotional differentiation. Visually appealing with a premium feel. Quick and engaging despite 5 questions. Personality result felt meaningful — reminiscent of Spotify Wrapped.

**Weaknesses:** Quiz may feel disconnected from the product experience — users feared answers wouldn't shape the app. Answer options feel too narrow and binary — users wanted multi-select or "other." May not resonate equally across genders — lower ratings mainly came from men, suggesting a more feminine feel in tone or visual language.

### Poll Concept
A 3-question flow that reveals how other users answered (percentage breakdowns).

**Strengths:** Short and easy. Percentages created a sense of community for some users.

**Weaknesses:** Percentages lacked clear purpose — felt like survey data, not a product experience. No users recognized the intended Instagram story-poll pattern. Questions too general and lacking depth. Most divisive overall (highest variance in ratings).

## Quantitative Results

| Question | Baseline Mean (SD) | Persona Mean (SD) | Poll Mean (SD) | p-value | Significant? |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|---------|-------------|
| Visual Appeal | 4.17 (0.93) | 4.35 (0.77) | 4.12 (0.94) | p = .562 | No |
| Engagement | 3.77 (1.07) | 4.28 (0.75) | 4.08 (0.94) | p = .107 | No |
| Scenario Fit | 4.22 (0.95) | 4.25 (0.93) | 3.77 (1.21) | p = .145 | No |

**No statistically significant differences** between any groups (all p > .05). Persona trends highest on engagement; Poll trends lowest on scenario fit. Would require larger samples to confirm.

**Variance insight:** Persona shows the tightest spread (SD 0.75-0.93) — users mostly agreed these ratings were high, suggesting a consistently positive experience. Poll's Scenario Fit has the widest spread (SD 1.21) — no consensus. Both concept-specific features were polarizing (Persona personality type SD = 1.29, Poll percentages SD = 1.37), but Poll was most divisive overall.

## Recommendations

1. **Persona concept is the strongest candidate** — created delight and interest. Refine the personality quiz with broader answer options and a clear explanation of how results impact the in-app experience.
2. **Add a visible personalization payoff** — in all three experiences, users wanted to see how their answers changed the app. A "based on your answers, we recommend..." moment would close the loop.
3. **Consider hybrid: Persona flow + Poll element** — showing social proof at the personality result ("38% of users share your type") could combine the best of both concepts.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Mixed methods — unmoderated usability testing + ranking
- **Participants:** 120 non-FT users (40 per concept), ages 18-40, 50/50 gender
- **Platform:** UserTesting.com with Figma prototypes
- **Measures:** Visual appeal, engagement, scenario fit (5-point scales), open-ended qualitative feedback
- **Analysis:** Kruskal-Wallis tests for statistical significance
- **Date:** April 2026
