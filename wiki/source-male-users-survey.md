# Source: Male Users Survey

**Date:** January 2026 | **Sample:** n=1,500 across Facetune, FaceApp, and non-editor users

Facetune and FaceApp male users follow identical editing patterns and differ significantly from non-editors. Two distinct segments exist within both apps: appearance-focused editors (skin, teeth, eyes) and overall-picture editors (background, composition). Non-FT male awareness is critically low (84.2% never heard or tried).

## Core Segments

**Segment 1: Personal Appearance-Focused** (Skin, teeth, eyes, face reshaping)
- FT users: High adoption of face-specific tools
- FaceApp users: Identical editing patterns
- Non-editors: Significantly lower interest (11–19pt gap)

**Segment 2: Overall Picture-Focused** (Background removal, composition, lighting)
- FT users: Secondary to appearance focus
- FaceApp users: Show same pattern
- Non-editors: Slightly higher interest but still below FT/FaceApp users

## Editing Priority Hierarchy (All Segments)

1. **Photo enhancement** — 58–76% across all groups (highest priority)
2. **Background editing** — 50–64% across all groups
3. **Appearance features** — 34–53% (highest variance between segments)
4. **Special effects** — 20–45% (interests vary)

## Gaming: The Wildcard

Gaming is the top interest category for male users — 39–45% across FT, FaceApp, and non-editors. Gaming interest does not predict photo editing behavior. Recommendation: separate gaming interest from editing motivation in messaging.

## Critical Market Gap: Awareness

84.2% of non-FT male users "never heard or tried" Facetune. This represents massive untapped audience among non-editors, but also indicates weak brand penetration in male segments. Perception barrier: app is seen as female-focused or for "face people" only.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey
- **Participants:** 1,500 male users split across three groups (500 each)
  - Facetune active users
  - FaceApp active users
  - Non-editor baseline users
- **Measures:** Feature interest, editing frequency, app awareness, intention to try
- **Date:** January 2026
