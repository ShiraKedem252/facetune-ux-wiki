# Source: Onboarding & First Experience

**Researcher:** Shira | **Date:** January 2025 | **Sample:** n=24 unmoderated usability tests

New users struggle with two critical barriers in their first session: the "+" button is invisible, and the paywall's value proposition fails to justify the subscription for users just exploring.

## Key Findings

**"+" Button Is Invisible**
New users scan the main editing screen and cannot locate the button to add/import a photo. The button's position, size, or visual treatment doesn't read as actionable. Users either give up or accidental discover it after 2-3 minutes of searching.

**Paywall Value Proposition Fails for Explorers**
When users hit the paywall on their first attempt to use a premium feature, the messaging doesn't convince them to subscribe. New users haven't experienced the value of Facetune yet — asking them to pay before exploring creates friction. Message positioning, feature highlights, and pricing all fall short.

## Impact

Both barriers create a conversion loss in the critical first 3 minutes of app experience. Nicole users (beauty-focused) abandon if they can't find the basic import button. Zoe users (authenticity-focused) reject the paywall without understanding the value. Jordan users (explorers) churn when feature access is gated.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Unmoderated usability tests
- **Participants:** 24 new Facetune users
- **Task:** "Edit a photo in Facetune as you normally would"
- **Measure:** Completion rates, time-to-first-feature, paywall interaction patterns
- **Duration:** Jan 2025

## Segment Impact

| Persona | Primary Barrier |
|---------|---|
| Nicole (Beauty) | Can't find import button; abandons |
| Zoe (Authentic) | Paywall perceived as cash grab; leaves |
| Jordan (Explorer) | Feature wall blocks exploration path |
