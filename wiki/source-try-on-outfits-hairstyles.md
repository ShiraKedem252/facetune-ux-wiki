# Source: Virtual Try-On for Outfits & Hairstyles

**Researcher:** Shira | **Date:** June 2025 | **Sample:** n=10 qualitative interviews

Users follow a predictable 5-stage purchase journey when evaluating outfits and hairstyles. Fit is the dominant consideration — more important than color, style, or brand. Virtual try-on solves the fit problem that prevents online clothing purchases.

## The 5-Stage Purchase Journey

1. **Inspiration** → User finds a look (TikTok, Instagram, Pinterest)
2. **Search** → Hunts for similar outfit or specific pieces
3. **Try-On** → Mentally simulates wearing it (this is where virtual try-on adds value)
4. **Fit Check** → Evaluates how it would look on their body
5. **Purchase Decision** → Commits based on fit confidence

## The Fit Problem

Users consistently cite fit as the **#1 barrier** to buying clothes online:
- "I need to see how it fits my shoulders"
- "Does this hide or show my stomach?"
- "Will the proportions work for my body type?"

Traditional product photos (flat lay, model) don't solve this. Virtual try-on directly addresses fit anxiety by showing the garment on the user's body type, in real light, with accurate proportions.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Qualitative interviews
- **Participants:** 10 shoppers (primarily women, ages 18-35)
- **Protocol:** "Think-aloud" as users searched for and evaluated outfits online
- **Context:** Fashion and hairstyle e-commerce
