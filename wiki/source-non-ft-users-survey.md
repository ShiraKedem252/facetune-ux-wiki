# Source: Non-Facetune Users Survey

**Researcher:** Shira | **Date:** November 2025 | **Sample:** n=536 across 4 demographic segments

Lighting is the universal editing need across all segments (69–85%), but Facetune is positioned as a face-reshaping app. Non-FT users rank body reshaping and face adjustments as lowest priorities. Awareness is critically low outside Gen Z females. Authenticity concerns spike with age (Millennials lead at 18% concern). Negative brand associations exist ("fake," "catfish").

## Editing Priorities Across All Groups

**Ranked by importance (% wanting this feature):**

| Rank | Feature | Range |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lighting/Color/Brightness | 69–85% |
| 2 | Filter/Preset Styles | 58–72% |
| 3 | Background Editing | 55–68% |
| 4 | Body Reshaping | 28–42% (LOWEST) |
| 5 | Face Adjustments | 31–45% (LOWEST) |

**Key insight:** Non-FT users want lighting and filters. Face/body reshaping is niche, not mass-market. Facetune's core positioning misaligns with majority needs.

## Segment Differences

**Gen Z Females** — Aware of FT; concerns about authenticity emerging  
**Millennials** — Highest authenticity anxiety (18%); avoid face-reshaping tools  
**Gen X** — Low awareness; practical interest in lighting and background editing  
**Men (all ages)** — Extremely low FT awareness; interested in photo enhancement but not appearance tools

## Brand Perception & Negative Associations

**12.9%** have negative word associations with photo editing apps:
- "Fake"
- "Catfish"
- "Artificial"
- "Vanity"
- "Deceptive"

Non-FT users conflate Facetune with extreme face-reshaping (perception issue, not product reality).

## Awareness Gap

- Gen Z females: Moderate awareness
- Outside Gen Z females: Critically low
- Negative perception ceiling: 12.9% hear "fake" before trying

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey
- **Participants:** 536 non-Facetune users across 4 demographic segments
  - Gen Z females (18–27)
  - Millennials (28–42)
  - Gen X (43–58)
  - Males (all ages, separate segment)
- **Measures:** Feature interest, FT awareness, brand perception, editing priorities, authenticity concerns
- **Date:** November 2025

## Implications

1. **Positioning mismatch** — Non-users want lighting; FT leads with face reshaping
2. **Awareness ceiling** — FT is invisible outside Gen Z female demographic
3. **Authenticity risk** — Repositioning toward enhancement (not transformation) is essential to reduce "fake" perception
