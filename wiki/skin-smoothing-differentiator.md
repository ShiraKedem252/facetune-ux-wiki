# Skin Smoothing: FT's Core Differentiator and the Authenticity Paradox

Skin smoothing is the one editing capability Facetune owns that users struggle to get elsewhere. Compared to non-FT users, FT users cite skin smoothing at **+27.6 percentage points** among Millennials, making it a true differentiator. Yet it's also heavily used by users who churn, caught in the authenticity tension.

## Why It's a Differentiator

**FT users (n=835) vs. non-FT users:** Existing users cite skin smoothing far more frequently. The gap is largest for Millennials (+27.6 pts), indicating the feature is genuinely valued by the segment most likely to care about smooth skin.

Competitors like Lightroom and VSCO have limited smoothing tools. CapCut and TikTok have smoothing (live filters), but not the precision FT offers. Native camera apps have minimal smoothing. **Skin smoothing is authentically FT's domain.**

This is why males keep FT in their app ecosystem — they know FT smooths skin better than anything else they have access to.

## The Usage-Churn Paradox

[[source-gen-z-churn]] shows that among churned users, **skin smoothing was the #1 most-used feature (42.8%)** before they left. They didn't churn because the feature didn't work — they churned despite heavy use of it.

Why? The authenticity problem. Users smoothed their skin, saw results that looked "too perfect" or "unrealistic" (34.3%), and decided they didn't want to look that edited. The feature itself created the problem that drove churn.

This reveals the core tension in [[authenticity-and-editing]]: smoothing capability ≠ authenticity value. More powerful tools create bigger authenticity anxiety.

## The Detection Anxiety Connection

Male FT users ([[source-male-first-insights]]) use smoothing but with restraint, driven by detection anxiety. They smooth skin but minimize the effect to avoid looking edited. The feature is used, but not maximally — held back by fear of detection.

## The Product Implications

The paradox is real: FT's differentiator creates the authenticity problem that drives churn. Doubling down on smoother skin won't solve retention. Instead:

1. **Reframe the tool:** "Natural skin enhancement" not "perfect skin flattening"
2. **Add controls for naturalism:** Intensity slider, graduated application, blending modes
3. **Pair with authenticity messaging:** "Keep it real" positioning around smoothing
4. **Use lighting alternatives:** [[lighting-universal-editing-need]] shows users want lighting options. Offering skin smoothing + natural lighting control gives users both — enhancement that feels real

Skin smoothing alone can't be the pillar anymore if the market is shifting authenticity-first.
