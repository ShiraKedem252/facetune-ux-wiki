# Source: AI Camera Proof-of-Concept

**Researcher:** (from docx) | **Date:** (date from study) | **Sample:** n=30 Gen Z users

The "photo rescue" value proposition resonates strongly with Gen Z. AI Camera concept testing shows high interest when framed as "fix bad shots in real-time." However, identity accuracy is the #1 pain point — users reject any AI camera feature that alters their face or appearance.

## Photo Rescue: The Winning Narrative

**"Rescue" Frame (High Adoption):**
- "Fix an underexposed shot before you leave"
- "Brighten that dark venue photo in real-time"
- "Enhance what's already there"

Users enthusiastically adopt AI Camera when positioned as a **rescue tool** — fixing bad conditions, not changing their appearance.

**"Transform" Frame (Low Adoption):**
- "Make yourself look better"
- "AI-enhanced beauty camera"
- "Look more attractive on video call"

Reframing as transformation/beautification drops interest, especially for [[persona-zoe-gen-z-selective]] and Gen Z users concerned with authenticity.

## Identity Accuracy: The #1 Blocker

Users cited facial identity as their primary concern:

- **"Will I look like myself?"** — Highest anxiety
- "Does it change my facial structure?" — Second concern
- "Does it alter my expression?" — Third concern

Even **subtle** identity shifts cause rejection. Users want:
- Same face, better lighting
- Same face, clearer skin
- Same face, brighter eyes
- NOT "enhanced face that looks vaguely like me"

This mirrors findings from [[source-reactions-retake-headshots]] and [[source-reactions-augmented-photography]].

## Use Cases with Highest Interest

1. **Video calling** — Fix bad lighting on Zoom/FaceTime (high interest)
2. **Event photos** — Rescue indoor/dark photos before sharing (high interest)
3. **Story posts** — Quick enhancement for Instagram Stories (moderate interest)
4. **Beauty transformation** — "Make myself look beautiful" (low interest)

The hierarchy is **rescue > enhancement > transformation**.

## Gen Z Segment Specifics

Gen Z users were 60% more likely to request identity assurance than Millennials. Messaging must emphasize "you, just better" — not "an improved version of you."

## Methodology

- **Type:** Qualitative proof-of-concept testing
- **Participants:** 30 Gen Z users (ages 18-27)
- **Protocol:** Demo AI Camera concept, reaction, pain points, use case interest
- **Focus:** Identity, authenticity, accuracy concerns
