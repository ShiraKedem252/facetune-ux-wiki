# Source: Male FT User In-Depth Interviews

> **Note:** This is a collection of individual qualitative interviews. Findings represent observed patterns from a small sample and should be cross-referenced with larger quantitative studies for validation.

**Sample:** n=4 in-depth interviews with male Facetune users | **Type:** Qualitative | **Date:** 2026

Four male FT users reveal a consistent pattern: detection anxiety drives every edit decision. FT carries social stigma that other editing apps don't. Users layer FT with Lightroom for "plausible deniability." Each user has a specific fix they want and then exits the app. Subscription is a barrier; trial-account workarounds are common. Editing for friends is a hidden social service.

## The Four Users

**Andrew** (27, PM)
Uses Lightroom presets as foundation. FT for teeth whitening only. Explicitly avoids reshaping — "too concerning." Prioritizes results that look undetectable. Leaves app after single feature use.

**Anthony** (22, Miami, Construction)
Edits friends' photos including body reshaping (on behalf of others). Feels Miami social pressure to look perfect. Keeps own FT use private. Has web design hobby; comfortable with design tools but treats FT as specialized tool, not creative platform.

**Cullen** (27, NYC, Data Analyst)
Free-trial workaround user — creates new email accounts to extend trial access rather than subscribe. Wants "does this look natural?" feature (feedback loop). Avoids any edit risking detection. Won't use reshaping tools.

**John** (27, PA Student, Chicago)
FT only for hand redness correction (medical, not cosmetic). Defines "natural" as no change to actual features. Prefers Lightroom as primary editor. Treats FT as specialist tool for specific problem, not general editor.

## Key Patterns

**Detection Anxiety Is Dominant**
All four users make every decision based on detectability. "Will people know I edited this?" is the first question. Reshaping tools are off-limits. Results must pass scrutiny.

**FT Carries Stigma; Other Apps Don't**
Lightroom, VSCO, CapCut have "plausible deniability" — they're framed as photography or design tools. FT is framed as appearance manipulation. The brand perception creates reluctance.

**Specific Fix, Then Exit**
Users open FT with a narrow goal (teeth, hand redness, lighting). They don't explore. They apply one feature and leave. No discovery, no secondary features.

**Subscription Fatigue**
Cullen's trial-account workaround is a sign. Paying for a niche tool (used 2x/month) feels unjustified. Cheaper one-time options are appealing.

**Social Editing Service**
Anthony edits for friends — a hidden social role. "I'll fix that for you" is acceptable; advertising personal use is not. This dual standard is worth exploring.

## Methodology

- **Type:** In-depth qualitative interviews
- **Participants:** 4 male Facetune users (ages 22–27, diverse geographies and professions)
- **Depth:** 30–45 minute conversations on editing philosophy, tool preferences, detection concerns, social norms
- **Gemini-transcribed:** Audio transcripts converted to text
