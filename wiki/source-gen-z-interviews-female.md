# Source: Gen Z Female In-Depth Interviews

> **Note:** This is a collection of individual qualitative interviews. Findings represent observed patterns from a small sample and should be cross-referenced with larger quantitative studies for validation.

**Sample:** n=7 in-depth interviews | **Type:** Qualitative | **Date:** 2026

Gen Z females edit strategically but don't all edit the same way. Three editing philosophies emerge: consistency seekers (maintain a cohesive feed identity), selective enhancers (edit before posting only), and time-invested perfectionist editors (1+ hour per post).

## The Seven Users

**Dani** (Content Creator, Houston)
Edits every post with consistent filter approach across feed for brand identity. Maintains visual cohesion as core practice.

**Ella** (20, College, Speech Pathology Major)
Edits every other post, primarily on Instagram. Selective approach to editing; not every moment gets edited.

**Caitlin** (Less Frequent Editor, Authenticity-Focused)
Reduced editing frequency over time. Uses VSCO and Peachy. Inspired by creators Spencer Barbosa and Victoria Garrick (natural aesthetic advocates). Values authenticity increasingly.

**Madison** (RN, RV Traveler)
Edits right before posting; follows natural aesthetic. Minimal intervention approach; focus on lighting rather than feature reshaping.

**Ava** (UVA Grad, Policy Work, Virginia/DC)
Perfectionist editor; invests ~1 hour per post. Creates photo dumps and collages. High editing investment per piece.

**Shaleyah** (22, DC, George Mason Grad, Sephora Beauty Advisor)
Editing = enhancement, not transformation. Fashion and makeup enthusiast. Reduced social media dependency overall. Distinguishes between "acceptable" minor edits and major alterations.

**Shayda** (Freelance Content Creator, DTC Wellness/Food Brands)
Uses Canva for fun stickers. Editing is enjoyable, not burdensome. Posts infrequently but with intention and effort. Treats editing as creative play.

## Key Insight

Editing frequency and investment vary wildly — not all Gen Z females edit heavily or frequently. The authenticity-natural aesthetic movement (Caitlin, Madison, Shaleyah) represents a real shift away from heavy filtering. Multi-app stacking is universal: no one uses Facetune alone. Time investment separates casual edits (Ella, Madison) from creative sessions (Ava, Shayda).

## Methodology

- **Type:** In-depth qualitative interviews
- **Participants:** 7 Gen Z female users (18–27 age range)
- **Depth:** 30–60 minute conversations on editing philosophy, app preferences, aesthetic goals, and social comparison
- **Geographic diversity:** Houston, DC/Virginia, college towns, RV travel
