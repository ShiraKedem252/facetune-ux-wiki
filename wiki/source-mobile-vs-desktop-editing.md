# Source: Mobile vs. Desktop Editing

**Researcher:** Shira | **Date:** April 2026 | **Sample:** n=1,438 (430 FT + 1,008 non-FT)

Photo editing is a cross-device activity with distinct use cases: phone for convenience (55%) and speed, desktop for precision (45–47%) and control. Desktop sessions run 30+ minutes for 33% of users; 82% of phone sessions finish within 30 minutes. Selfies and portraits strongly favor phone (+38pp); professional content strongly favors desktop (+23pp). FT users do more beauty-specific edits on both devices. Male users lean desktop more than females. 14% start on phone, finish on desktop.

## Device Preference by Task

**Phone Dominates**
- Selfies and portraits (+38pp vs. desktop)
- Quick touch-ups
- Social posting context

**Desktop Dominates**
- Professional content (+23pp vs. phone)
- High-precision work
- Batch processing
- Layout and design work

## Session Duration: The 30-Minute Threshold

| Device | 30+ Minutes | Under 30 Minutes |
|---|---|---|
| Desktop | 33% | 67% |
| Phone | 18% | 82% |

Mobile sessions are quick hits; desktop sessions are sustained work.

## Precision Gap: The #1 Phone Complaint

**14% of phone users** report lack of precision as their top complaint. Desktop offers finer controls, pinch-to-zoom, multiple monitors, and larger screens. For detail work (blemish removal, fine retouching), phone is frustrating.

## Beauty-Specific Editing Skews FT

FT users do more appearance-focused edits on both devices compared to non-FT users. Desktop + FT is particularly high — users leverage precision for detailed face/skin work.

## Gender Difference: Males Lean Desktop

| Gender | Phone | Desktop |
|---|---|---|
| Female | 2.9x editing | 2.9x editing |
| Male | 2.7x editing | 3.7x editing |

Males are disproportionately higher on desktop. Potential drivers: gaming PC culture, creative tool familiarity, or detection anxiety (desktop = privacy).

## Cross-Device Workflow: 14% Switch Mid-Session

14% start editing on phone, then switch to desktop to finish. This hybrid workflow suggests:
- Phone = initial capture/quick adjustments
- Desktop = refinement and precision work

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey + behavior tracking
- **Participants:** 1,438 total (430 Facetune + 1,008 non-FT users)
- **Measures:** Device preference by task type, session duration, precision concerns, cross-device workflows
- **Date:** April 2026

## Recommendations

**For Desktop FT:**
- High-precision appearance tools
- Clean, uncluttered UI (room for controls)
- Support batch processing for multiple photos

**For Mobile FT:**
- Speed-first UX (quick presets, single-tap edits)
- Simplify controls for small screens
- Enable phone-to-desktop sync for deeper work

**For Cross-Device:**
- Save progress across devices
- One-click switch between devices
- Cloud sync for non-destructive editing
