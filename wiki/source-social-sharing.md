# Source: Social Sharing & Comfort Levels

**Researcher:** Shira | **Date:** March 2025 | **Sample:** n=100 survey

Sharing comfort decreases sharply with social distance. Users rate sharing with close circles 4.2-4.3/5 (high comfort) but drop to 3.2/5 with acquaintances. **72.4% of shares happen via private messaging apps**, not social feeds — users prefer intimate channels for edited photos.

## Comfort by Audience Distance

| Audience | Comfort Rating | Example |
|----------|---|---|
| **Close circle** | 4.2-4.3/5 | Close friends, family, partner |
| **General followers** | 3.5/5 | Instagram followers, secondary network |
| **Acquaintances** | 3.2/5 | Coworkers, classmates, loose connections |
| **Public/Strangers** | 1.8/5 | Public feed, TikTok, complete strangers |

The gap between close circles (4.2) and acquaintances (3.2) is **1 full point** — the difference between "I'm comfortable" and "I'm hesitant."

## The Messaging Apps Dominance

**72.4% of Facetune-edited photos are shared via:**
- WhatsApp
- iMessage
- Signal
- Telegram
- Direct messages on social platforms

Only **27.6% shared on public feeds** (Instagram, TikTok, etc.).

**Why private channels win:**
- Edited photos feel more personal, less performative
- Users control the audience (not algorithm)
- Intimacy reduces anxiety about over-editing
- Messaging is how close friends normally exchange photos

## Segment Differences

[[persona-nicole-beauty-driven-millennial]] — High comfort in close circles, hesitant with acquaintances. The more "enhanced" the edit, the more she restricts audience.

[[persona-zoe-gen-z-selective]] — Even lower public comfort; strong preference for messaging. Minimal edits reduce but don't eliminate audience anxiety.

[[persona-alex-influencer-business]] — Inverted pattern. High comfort sharing on public feeds (it's her business); uses editing to amplify, not hide.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey
- **Participants:** 100 Facetune users
- **Measures:** Comfort ratings (1-5 scale) by audience type, channel preference, frequency
- **Date:** March 2025
