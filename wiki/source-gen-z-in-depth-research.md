# Source: Gen Z In-Depth Research

**Date:** December 2025 | **Sample:** n=6 in-depth interviews | **Status:** Preliminary findings

⚠️ **Note:** This report is marked "NOT READY YET!!!" — findings are indicative but not finalized.

Two distinct archetypes drive Gen Z editing behavior: Curation Seekers (build a cohesive feed identity) and Expressive Artists (use editing as creative play). Both view photo editing as core to self-expression and social presentation. Repetitive manual tasks create friction; fear of appearing inauthentic creates decision paralysis.

## Two Archetypes

**Curation Seeker**
Treats Instagram/TikTok feed as a curated gallery. Edits to maintain visual cohesion, consistent color palette, and brand identity. Spending 30–60 minutes on a single post is normal. Primary pain: manual repetition across multiple photos.

**Expressive Artist**
Views editing as creative experimentation and play. Less concerned with feed cohesion; more interested in visual surprise, humor, and self-expression. Edits are personal, not performance. Pain: wants creative control without technical barriers.

## Key Tensions

**Self-Expression vs. Inauthenticity Fear**
Both archetypes want to edit but fear appearing "fake" or "filtered." They distinguish between enhancement (okay) and transformation (concerning). The line is subjective and emotionally fraught.

**Creative Intent vs. Grunt Work**
Editing software handles basic tasks (skin smoothing, lighting) but requires manual labor. Users want creative control over the final vision without tedious clicking, dragging, and adjusting.

## Lighting Keyword Finding

Lighting is the universal first step for both archetypes — both during photo capture and post-editing. Good lighting solves 70% of the problem. Editing tools that enhance lighting (not just apply filters) resonate.

## Editing Stack

All participants use 2–4 apps per photo: TikTok, Instagram, Canva, VSCO, Peachy, Lightroom, and occasionally Facetune. No single app owns the workflow. App role depends on content type and editing goal.

## Methodology

- **Type:** In-depth qualitative interviews
- **Participants:** 6 Gen Z users (18–27)
- **Depth:** 45–90 minute conversations on editing philosophy, creative goals, pain points, app preferences
- **Scope:** Exploratory; findings are preliminary
- **Date:** December 2025

## Recommendations (Preliminary)

1. **Reframe appearance tools** — Position as enhancement tools, not transformation ("bring out your best light" not "change your face")
2. **Batch editing with signature style** — Apply filters/adjustments to multiple photos at once to reduce repetition
3. **Make editing fun** — Emphasize experimentation and creative play over flawless results
