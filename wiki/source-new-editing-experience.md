# Source: New Editing Experience (EX/Genie Study)

**Sample:** n=22 unmoderated usability tests | **Date:** 2026

The new AI editor generates strong first impressions. Text input is valued. The Mark feature is overlooked. Loading times cause feature skips. Users expect the AI to auto-complete edits without additional prompts.

## Key Findings

**Strong First Impression**
AI editing immediately signals "this is different." Users perceive value in automation and reduced manual work.

**Text Input is Valued**
Describing what you want ("make me look less tired") feels more intuitive than button clicking. Conversational input resonates with users accustomed to generative AI.

**Mark Feature Is Overlooked**
The Mark feature (manual region selection) is not discovered or used. Users skip it; they expect the AI to handle specificity without painter-like tools.

**Loading Times Cause Feature Skips**
When AI takes more than 2–3 seconds to process, users abandon the feature mid-session. Speed is feature.

**Expectation: Auto-Completion**
Users expect the AI to understand intent and complete edits without follow-up prompts. One-shot prompting feels incomplete if it requires additional refinement.

## Methodology

- **Type:** Unmoderated usability testing
- **Participants:** 22 users (mix of existing Facetune users and new testers)
- **Task:** Explore and edit a photo using the new EX/Genie AI editor
- **Measure:** Feature discovery, perceived value, friction points, time-to-abandon
- **Platform:** Mobile app

## Impact for Teams

- **Product:** Prioritize speed over feature richness; Mark discovery requires redesign
- **Marketing:** Lead with "describe your edit" messaging; AI understands intent
- **Design:** Multi-step refinement workflows will lose users; aim for single-prompt success
