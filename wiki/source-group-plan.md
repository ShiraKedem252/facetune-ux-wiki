# Source: Group Plan Research

**Date:** January 2026 | **Sample:** n=200 Facetune users, survey

Two-thirds of Facetune users discuss the app with close friends and family, but the "don't ask, don't tell" mindset dominates: they use it privately without advertising usage. A group plan appeals to 31.5% ("could be relevant"), but network awareness and privacy concerns create friction. Cost savings is the primary value driver.

## Usage Sharing Behavior

**66%** discuss or share Facetune use with close friends  
**47%** mention it to family members  
**"Don't ask, don't tell" norm** — Most users are quiet about their own usage but aware others edit too

## Distinction: Acceptable vs. Major Edits

Users categorize editing on a spectrum:
- **Minor/acceptable edits** — Skin smoothing, lighting, teeth whitening, light reshaping
- **Major alterations** — Face restructuring, extreme body changes, unrecognizable transformations

Group plan acceptance depends on shared understanding that "acceptable" edits are normal. Perception of others' edits as "extreme" reduces group plan appeal.

## Group Plan Relevance

| Response | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Not relevant | 20.5% |
| Could be relevant | 31.5% |
| Uncertain | 48% |

**Uncertain segment (48%)** is the key opportunity — they can be moved with right messaging.

## Current Group Subscription Baseline

**35%** already use group plans for other services (Netflix, Spotify, Apple One). Familiarity exists but mental model may not transfer to beauty/editing apps.

## Value Drivers

1. **Cost savings** — Primary motivation (split subscription across group)
2. **Convenience** — One shared account reduces setup friction
3. **Social alignment** — "We all do this together" normalizes the behavior

## Barriers

1. **Network awareness** — Don't know which friends/family members would benefit
2. **Privacy concerns** — Uncomfortable with shared account or usage visibility
3. **Unclear value** — Uncertain what features justify cost for personal use, let alone shared

## Methodology

- **Type:** Quantitative survey
- **Participants:** 200 active Facetune users
- **Questions:** Sharing behavior, group plan interest, current group subscriptions, barriers, value drivers
- **Date:** January 2026
